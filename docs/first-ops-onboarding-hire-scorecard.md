# First OpSpot ops/onboarding QA operator — scorecard

Updated: 2026-05-08T23:10:00Z

## Why this hire exists
OpSpot needs one human operator who makes agent work feel trustworthy before customers see it. They do not replace agents; they QA outputs, chase missing onboarding info, keep clients calm, and escalate approval moments to Colton.

## Outcomes in first 14 days
- Every new customer has a complete intake packet or a clear missing-info list within 24 hours.
- Every agent-produced customer-facing artifact is checked against a simple QA checklist before approval.
- Colton gets one daily queue summary: decisions needed, blocked customers, money/close opportunities.
- No external customer action ships without explicit approval.

## Must-have traits
- Polished and trustworthy in customer-facing writing/calls.
- Detail-obsessed: enjoys checklists, receipts, and follow-through.
- Calm under ambiguity; does not panic when an automation breaks.
- AI-curious and willing to work beside agents instead of competing with them.
- Business mature: understands that speed matters, but trust matters more.

## Avoid
- Hiring primarily for appearance, age, gender, or “vibe.” Those are unreliable and create risk.
- Pure social-media/operator energy without checklist discipline.
- Anyone who wants to invent process before they can run the existing queue.

## Compensation test range
- Trial project: $75–$150 for a 2–3 hour paid task.
- Part-time starter: $18–$30/hr depending on polish and autonomy.
- First month cap: 5–10 hrs/week until the queue proves repeatable.

## Paid test task
Give candidate a mock customer onboarding folder and three agent outputs. Ask them to:
1. Find missing onboarding information.
2. QA the agent outputs against the checklist.
3. Draft a calm customer follow-up message.
4. Produce a one-page queue summary for Colton.

Pass if they are concise, accurate, calm, and catch obvious gaps without overcomplicating.

## Sourcing channels
- Local Wilmington entrepreneur/student network.
- 1MC / coworking / Lumos-adjacent intros.
- Trusted friend-of-founder referrals.
- Small paid test post for “AI operations assistant / customer onboarding QA.”

## First outreach message
“Hey — I’m looking for a sharp part-time operator for OpSpot, my AI employees company in Wilmington. The role is customer onboarding QA: reviewing AI-generated work, catching missing info, and keeping clients calm while we build automations. Starts with a paid 2–3 hour test project, then 5–10 hrs/week if it clicks. You’d need to be organized, polished with customers, and curious about AI. Interested?”

## Approval rule
This person may draft and prepare, but cannot send customer-facing messages, change customer accounts, or approve automation actions without Colton’s approval until explicitly promoted.
